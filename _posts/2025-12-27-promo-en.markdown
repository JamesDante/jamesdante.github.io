*Principles of Marketing* has done a remarkable job of framing and systematizing contemporary commercial marketing behavior. In practice, the meaning of "marketing" keeps shifting with the environment — you can see this reflected in the book's ongoing revisions — evolving from one-way broadcasting into what is today a process of co-creation with users.

There's a deep social transformation behind this. Products are multiplying. Channels are changing faster than ever. Even niche markets aren't so niche anymore.

If you strictly follow marketing principles — no matter how precisely you identify competitors, how perfectly you position yourself, how user-centered your product is, how attractive your pricing — stitching these functional components together still tends to leave people feeling like they're being treated as wallets.

Modern marketing, then, is something else entirely: **finding kindred souls in a noise-saturated market**. If you genuinely find those kindred souls, doesn't connection, value exchange — all of it — follow naturally?
